Leveraging the alpha consumer

We all can’t be experts on everything. Even casual, hobbyist experts. We all have our own unique interests. And thank goodness, or what a mundane world this would be. A real yawner. But because we can’t be experts on everything, when we get into a situation that is outside of our personal expertise, we typically opt for the “phone a friend” and seek out the people who know what they are talking about.

We all have these people in our lives. The friend who has a green thumb who we consult before buying any new garden supplies. The wine aficionado who always has the best recommendations for a great wine at a great price. The DIY-er who has tried every brand of paint, so you don’t have to. Or the health nut that can quickly tell you the best brand of almond milk or coconut water. These are the people that we go to first for their specialized information and expertise.

This everyday practice can and should be leveraged in marketing strategy. When determining a demographic for a brand, it just makes sense to find and take advantage of that slice of the consumer pie who are fanatics about the category. They exist in every category and have the power to exercise a disproportionate impact on business results, not only through purchase volume but also through their influence over others. And they actually drive real business results. In fact, According to Harvard Business Review, in any product category, roughly 10% of the consumers account for more than 50% of the profits. That’s some serious influence.

We call these consumers Alpha Consumers™. And they are the key.

How do you find them?

In 2008 we commissioned Hall+Partners to help us create the Alpha Identifier Model. It’s a proprietary research study and segmentation tool that allows us to identify the Alpha Consumer in any category by incorporating the dimensions of financial and social worth.

Specifically we look at three core variables:

  • Passion: They are highly engaged in the category, often early adopters and innovators
  • Volume: They buy more than the typical customer
  • Influence: They are the person others seek out for category information and advice

Once identified, we then spend time getting to know their culture inside and out, using ethnographic research methods. We often create online consumer panels and incorporate the Alpha audience into our creative process, using their needs and desires as our guide. These passionate consumers inspire us, leading to new sources of engagement and more culturally relevant ideas. Because Alphas are so zealous about the category, they are in tune with trends and emerging ideas. A solid understanding of them allows the brand to act more opportunistically and stay ahead of the curve.

The Alphas represent the first eggs, not all the eggs, in our marketing basket.

The Alpha Consumer opportunity is best understood within a holistic targeting framework.

We certainly aren’t suggesting that you ignore Heavy Users. These frequent buyers are an important source of volume. At the other side of the spectrum are the Category Influentials. These individuals aren’t a huge source of volume, but are important from a cache and thought leadership perspective. Whether it’s engaging the celebrity of Kim Kardashian or 10 local food bloggers, we understand the power that their endorsement can bring. And even Inactives can be an audience if we are trying to bring new users into a category.

But our focus, where we tell our clients to spend their first dollars is on that Category Alpha. Here’s why. We know from working with a diverse group of clients across multiple categories and industries, that the fastest way to drive business success is to connect with and convert those Category Alphas into Brand Activists.

Alphas amplify our message and accelerate our results in three key ways:

  • INSIGHT – Because of their category involvement, Alphas are an important source of inspiration and insight. They help us get smart, and stay smart, about the category fast. They also can tell us where the conversations are happening and help us understand what’s influential.
  • PREFERENCE – Because they are always growing their category experience, they are easier to convert with the right idea. From a business perspective this translates into building penetration and brand preference more quickly.
  • INFLUENCE – Because of their social currency they can influence others through word-of-mouth, or by example, helping to accelerate brand awareness and trial.

"In other words, Alphas are the key to launching or growing an idea exponentially faster and more efficiently. They lead to better ideas and faster results."

What’s the best way to engage with my Alpha Consumers?

It really depends on a variety of factors and category dynamics. That’s the beauty of an Alpha Identifier Model research study – you can get very specific insights and actionable opportunities based on your category and brand. That said, here are a few key engagement strategies that we’ve found to be critical for communication success.

Give them something to believe in and belong to. Tap into shared values and aspirations to create a more meaningful and relevant connection by taking a stand. Craft a brand point-of-view that’s bigger than a singular product or service. Hint: this is about having a position, not just a positioning, in the marketplace.

Social currency is a huge motivator. Our research tells us that the Alpha is often driven by a desire to be viewed as someone “in the know.” They want social credit for having found something of value. Help them fulfill this desire by making your ideas portable and shareable. Ask them to participate and have them help create the story with you.

Build a role for the Alpha to play in the story of the brand. Ask the Alpha Consumers to participate and have them help create the story with you. People trust other people more than they trust brands, so their involvement is a powerful way to create a highly motivated community working to energize your brand’s idea.

Know the emotional reasons driving their category passion. You can’t harness what you don’t understand. It’s not enough to know who they are what they buy. It’s the why that makes the difference. Everything we do as humans is driven by a desire to feel a certain way. And by tapping into those shared values and consumer aspirations, our messages become more meaningful and contagious.

Applying these learnings is the difference between putting out an ad and building a connected, idea-driven community of Brand Activists.
